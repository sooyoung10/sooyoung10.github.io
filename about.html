“remember the kim family motto: to be able to survive anywhere no matter what,” my dad reminded me many times.

since i was a little boy, i’ve been “training” all my life to survive anywhere no matter what.
the sahara? no problem: my family never turned on the air conditioner in case we move to a scorching desert.
the himalayas? all prepared: going on 9 mile hikes has been a staple of the kim family vacations.
a tsunami? no sweat: ever since running a 5 km marathon as a 4 year old, i’ve trained enough to outrun it...
or, at least, this is what my parents claimed whenever i complained about our “training.”

back then, i didn’t understand why we had to train for such extreme situations because those were not in our foreseeable future.

but looking back now, the seemingly pointless training prepared me with the mental and physical strength to overcome any adversities i may face.

now i can survive anywhere no matter what.
